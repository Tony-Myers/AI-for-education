AI in Research: An Introduction

Artificial intelligence is rapidly becoming an indispensable tool in academic research, serving not as a replacement for human intellect, but as a powerful accelerator for discovery and analysis. By augmenting the researcher's capabilities, AI streamlines workflows, uncovers novel connections in data, and facilitates new modes of inquiry across all disciplines.

From the initial stages of literature synthesis and grant proposal writing, through to complex data analysis and the ethical drafting of manuscripts, AI offers support throughout the research lifecycle. It can help identify funding opportunities, perform preliminary coding of qualitative data, debug statistical scripts, and assist in disseminating findings to both academic and public audiences.

Harnessing this potential requires a commitment to responsible and ethical use. Effective integration depends on the core skills of AI literacy: critical evaluation of outputs, robust prompt engineering, and an awareness of data privacy.
